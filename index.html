Our Vision

"Starting The Cycle" means creating a cycling culture.

 
Start the Cycle is a social entrepreneurship venture founded by two McMaster Students – PhD Candidate Charles Burke and Masters of Science Candidate Justin Hall – each with research interests that fall in the field of transportation planning for active mobility. In short Start the Cycle operates a non-profit ‘bike share’ program for school aged children between the ages of 5 and 15 based on the same for profit bike share model used to promote bicycle use for transport in cities across Canada and the United States. The 2015 Pan American games Cannon Street bike path project provides an ideal opportunity to form a pilot project for Start the Cycle as it coincides with available funding and existing new infrastructure plans.

Sharing:
part of growing up

Bike sharing is an intuitive model that allows everyone access to cycling. Bike sharing is a way to service the most youths with the optimal number of bikes.

Universal Access:
social justice

Currently cycling infrastructure is being used by a narrow demographic. Our project aims to allow children of all backgrounds access to active transportation.

Benefits
of cycling

Cycling has many benefits: reducing childhood obesity, improves grades, fosters responsibility, increases self esteem. In general, cycling improves health, reduces emissions, stimulates local economies, and creates community identity.

Appealing
to youth

Children who cycle for transportation become adults who cycle for transportation. Recent studies have shown that the best way to stimulate a cycling culture is to make youth the focal point.

Pilot Project: Cannon Street, Hamilton, ON.
 
The city of Hamilton is creating a bike path along Cannon street, connecting Hess to Sherman. This bike path will facilitate activate transportation between Pan American and para-Pan American games venues. Start the cycle has been tasked with creating a positive legacy for this transport infrastructure.
